Theory

  • A particle is said to be in uniform circular motion if it travels in a circle (or arc) with constant speed v.
  • Acceleration (ac)(a_c)(ac​) is constant.
  • The velocity vector (v)(v)(v) is tangential to the circle. Its direction is constantly changing.
  • The magnitude of the velocity vector is constant, which means that the speed is constant.
  • The force (FcF_cFc​) is directed towards the center of the circle.
  • The period (T)(T)(T) is the tiem for one complete revolution.
  • The frequency (f)(f)(f) is defined as the number of revolutions in a second.
