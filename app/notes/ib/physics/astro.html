Stellar Quantities

  • Solar System: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune.

  • Interstellar: It is referred to as a the space between stars, roughly 5-20 Light-years(Ly) apart.

  • Stellar Cluster: It is a group of stars that are born out of the same clouds of gas and that exert a gravitational force on each other. There are 2 types of Stellar clusters:

  • Globular: A group of small numbers of old stars.

  • Open: A group of large numbers of newly formed young stars.

  • Constellations: A group of a small number of stars spread in a specific pattern.

  • Galaxy: A large group of stars that can contain around 10^n numbers of stars. There are around 200 billion Galaxies.

  • Types of Galaxies:

  • Spiral: Names of the galaxy speaks for itself

  • Elliptical:

  • Irregular:

  • Local Group: A group of ~20 Galaxies that are relatively close together.

  • Intergalactic Space: Space between galaxies usually ~100Ly
