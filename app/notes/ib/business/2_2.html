Organisational Structure

The way in which the company is organised and the consideration of its internal workings in an attempt to find an efficient operational practice

Why it is important-

Provides accountability (who is answerable for a specific job) and responsibility (who is in charge of whom)

Delegation

Extent a superior passes control and authority down the hierarchy to subordinates

  • Can motivate and develop employees while saving time for managers
  • May lead to confusion and inadequacy (in case of failure)
  • This includes accountability (for the employee) but responsibility still stays with higher authority (the line manager)

Span of Control

Refers to the number of people who are directly accountable to a manager, and it affects whether an organisation is wide/flat or narrow/tall

Span of control depends on - (MOST)

  • Manager’s experience, competence, traits
  • Organizational Culture
  • Skills and dynamics of subordinates (better team, less people)
  • Task (urgent tasks, narrower span)

Wide/Flat Organisations

  • Direct communication between different levels (fast and accurate)
  • Cost control (less managers needed)
  • Delegation is more important
  • Longer decision making
  • Eliminate feeling of alienation of workers from senior management

Narrow/Tall Organisations

  • Easier to control smaller amount of subordinates
  • May be more productive/efficient (team cohesiveness and specialization)
  • Fast communication within team
  • More costly (more managers needed)
  • More motivation for employees – many promotion opportunities

Levels of Hierarchy

Hierarchy refers to the organisational structure based on a ranking system. Its benefits include:

  • Shows clear lines of communication
  • Establish departments or teams (motivation and sense of belonging)

However,

  • Rivalries may occur
  • Rigid in terms of scope and authority
  • Departmentalisation leads to isolation of workers to their teams

Chain of command

Formal line of authority through which orders/ commands are passed down in an organisation. Businesses with few levels of hierarchy have shorter chains of command.

Delayering

Process of removing levels in the hierarchy/reducing managerial levels to achieve a flatter structure.

(Downsizing is when the size of the core workforce is reduced)

Advantages:

  • Reduce costs
  • Improve speed of communication
  • Encourage delegation

Disadvantages:

  • Can cause job insecurity, demotion, redundancy
  • Overloading of employees
  • Larger teams take decisions slower

Bureaucracy

Execution of tasks that are governed by official administrative and formal rules of an organisation. It involves clear division of roles for a hierarchical system in the organisation and follows these principles-

  • Prioritisation of continuity (less risk)
  • Rules and regulations
  • Formal hierarchy
  • Accountability

Advantages:

  • Authority and levels of responsibility are obvious
  • Standardization of processes to ensure efficiency

Disadvantages:

  • May stifle creativity
  • Rivalries between departments may ensue
  • Less job satisfaction; high labor turnover rate
  • Slow decision-making process
  • Salaries for the different layers of management increases costs

Centralisation and Decentralisation

Discusses the extent to which decision-making power is in the hands of the people of the firm.

Centralised Structures:

  • Executive board handles major decision making of the company
  • Majority of decision making by minority (senior mgmt.)

Advantages:

  • Better control over all company activity
  • Rapid decision making on single projects
  • Better sense of direction
  • Decisions are more consistent

Disadvantages:

  • Slow decision making on multiple projects
  • Stress for senior staff
  • Inflexible
  • Demotivating
  • Exclusion of other people’s ideas that may be better

Decentralised Structures

  • Some decision making is delegated
  • Freer communication process
  • All employees get to have a say in the decisions

Advantages:

  • Speedier decision making
  • Input from employees
  • Improved Morale
  • Accountability
  • Teamwork

Disadvantages:

  • No control
  • Greater chance of mistakes
  • Reliance on communication
  • Redundancy
  • Lower standards of work (no governing body)
  • Inconsistency between company goals (regional managers)

How should a company decide whether to centralise or decentralise?

  • Size of the organization
  • Scale of importance of decision
  • Level of risk
  • Corporate culture
  • Management attitude and competencies
  • Use of technology

Organisational Charts

An organization chart is a diagrammatic representation of a firm’s formal structure. It shows the different departments within a business, chain of command, span of control, levels of hierarchy and channels of communication.

Tall/Vertical Organization Charts

Narrower span of control, longer chain of command, more levels of hierarchy, more centralized, more bureaucratic

Flat/Horizontal Organization Charts

Wider span of control, shorter chain of command, fewer levels of hierarchy, more decentralized

Organizational structure can be determined by:

  • Product
  • Function
  • Region

Product-Based Organization

Is where human resources are organized around particular projects. Matrix structure is a flexible organization structure of representatives from different departments temporarily working together on a particular project.

Handy’s Shamrock Organization

People have to be satisfied through job enrichment and flexible practices.

3 main groups of staff-

Core Staff

Full-time professionals such as mangers and technicians who help in the day to day functioning of the firm. With advancements in tech, the core staff is becoming a smaller group which implies downsizing.

Peripheral Workers/ Contingent Workforce

These people are employed as and when needed. They are paid the flexible workforce of the organization which help in bringing the labor costs down. Larger businesses have more peripheral workers.

Outsourced workers

These are individuals who are not employed by the business but are paid to finish particular tasks. They are hired for their expertise. Eg. freelance workers, advertising agencies.

Communication

Transfer of information from one party to another

Electronic Mail

Process of using computer wide area networks (WAN) as a mailing system.

Mobile Devices

Such as smartphones for faster and more effective communication

Video Conferencing

Combination of mobile, computer and video technology.
