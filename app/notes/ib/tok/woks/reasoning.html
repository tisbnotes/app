Reasoning as a way of knowing

Deduction Induction
Reasoning from general to particular Reasoning from particular to general
Ex: All metals expand when heated, A is a metal, therefore A expands when heated EX: Metal A expands when heated so does metal D and C. Therefore all metals expand when heated

Logical Fallacies

Fallacy Example/meaning
Hasty Generalisation Generalising from insufficient evidence
Post hoc ergo propter hoc Since event Y is followed by X, Y must have been caused by X
Ad hominen Attacking/Supporting the person rather than the argument
Circular Reasoning Assuming the truth of what you are supposed to be proving
Special Pleading Using double standards to excuse an individual or group
Ad ignorantium Claiming something is true because it cannot be proven false
Equivocation Using language ambigiously
False analogy Assuming that because two things are alike in one way that they are alike in other aspects
False Dillema Assuming that only a limited number of alternatives exist
Loaded Question A question that is biased because it contains a built-in assumption
