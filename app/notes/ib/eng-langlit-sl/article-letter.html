Article

Features

  • will have a semi-formal to formal register and will identify the reader – an article is like a direct conversation with the reader; everything you write must speak to that reader and engage their interest right from the first sentence
  • will have a tone appropriate to task (suitable serious) – at the same time, avoid language that is too formal and more suited to essays; avoid words like ‘to sum up’, ‘some people say’, ‘nevertheless’, ‘on one hand’, etc
  • will have a relevant headline/title – use subheadings to break up the text and make clear paragraphs
  • will have an introduction intended to catch the readers’ attention – essentially ‘clickbaiting’, all the writer is trying to do is make you open the page to read their article
  • will use techniques that engage and interest readers – direct address, anecdotes, humour, pathos
  • can be argumentative persuasive or opinionated
  • will write an impactful ending - in an article, endings generally asking the reader another question or give a call to action

Letter to the editor/open letter

Features

  • reference to the original article or event
  • brevity
  • rhetorical questions
  • interesting pacts or quotations
  • a counter argument
  • a recommendation
  • a clear statement of opinion
  • the effect of the original article on you and others
  • emotive (at the risk of hyperbole)
  • punchy, concise sentences
