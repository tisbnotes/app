Molecules to Metabolism

Carbon Compounds

  • Carbon can form tetravalent covalent bonds - valency of 4
  • allows to form compounds with multiple elements to allow for a wide array of organic molecules
  • can form long chains that build into complex structures
  • single, double or triple bonds.
  • 4 main groups of organic compounds

Carbohydrates: composed of carbon, hydrogen and oxygen. Hydrogen and Oxygen are always in the ratio of two H to one O, like water (therefore hydrate).

Lipids: broad classification for all substances insoluble in water. Includes fats (solid at rtp) and oils (liquid at rtp).

Proteins: composed of chains of amino acids. Amino acids are units made up of carbon, hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen and occasionally sulphur.

Nucleic Acids: chains of subunits called nucleotides, made out of phosphorus, oxygen, carbon, hydrogen and oxygen. There are two types: ribonucleic acid (RNA) and deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA)

Metabolism

refers to all the different chemical reactions that occur within a cell to sustain life by transforming molecules

Anabolism

synthesis of larger, complex molecules from smaller, simple units
require energy in terms of ATP
E.g: protein synthesis at ribosomes, DNA synthesis during replication, photosynthesis

Catabolism

breaking down of larger, complex molecules to form smaller, simple units
releases energy, which is sometimes captured in the form of ATP
E.g. chemical digestion of food in the mouth or small intestine, cellular respiration
