Significant figures

Rules

  • In an integer, all digits count, except for:
    • Zeros at the end
    • Zeros in front
  • In a decimal, all digits after first digit in the the decimal count.

Rounding

  • When the last digit is >5>5>5, round up.
  • When the last digit is <5<5<5, round down.
  • When the last digit is 555:
    • Round down if the second last digit is even.
    • Round up if the second last digit is odd.

Math with significant figures

  • Addition/subtraction: The answer should be expressed with the same number of decimal points as the number with the least decimal points in the problem.

  • Multiplication/division: The answer should be expressed with the same number of significant figures as the number with the least significant figures in the problem.

Uncertainties

Types of uncertainty

Systematic Error

  • Biases measurements in the same direction
  • Due to both the observer and the instrument being used
  • Decreases accuracy of the measurement

Random Uncertainties

  • Repeated measurements of the same quantitiy show a spread of values, some too large, some too small.
  • Not biased in any direction
  • Decreases precision of the measurement

Accuracy and precision

  • A measurement is accurate if the systematic error is small.
  • It is precise if the random uncertainty is small

Determinants of uncertainty

  • Random errors in the measurements
    1. Reaction time of the experimenter
    2. Slight variation of external conditions
    3. Electrical noise in the instruments
  • Precision of the instrument
    1. Graduations on an analogue instrument
    2. Smallest value of a digital instrument
