Procedure

  1. People are told that the study concerns “how people think about and remember stories”
  2. The participants are assigned one of two roles; Homebuyer or Burglar
  3. The participants are asked to read a passage of 373 words. They are given two minutes to do so.
  4. There is a vocabulary test for 12 minutes that serves as a filler.
  5. Two blank pages are handed out and the participants are asked to recall their perspective in the story as accurately as possible.
  6. Participants have another filler task, but this time it is five minutes long.
  7. Participants have the choice to change perspectives or keep the same perspective.
  8. They are asked to recall the passage again.

The Results:

  • The people remembered more about their own roles in the first recollection trial.
  • People who changed perspective recalled an additional 7.1% information on the other perspective.
  • People who didn’t change their perspective recalled 2.9% less.

Conclusion:

  • Schemas organize the knowledge in our memory. New information is perceived through existing schemas. They even act when we are storing and recollecting information from Long Term Memory.
