Murdock (1962):

Aim:

  • To find a trend in how people remembered a set of words

Procedure:

Participants are asked to learn a list of words that varied from 10 â€“ 40 words.

  • Each word is presented for 2 seconds.

Results:

  • Probability of recalling word depended on its position
  • Words either early or at the end were recalled more often (known as serial position effect)

Conclusion:

  • Words early in the list were put into Long-Term Memory (primacy effect) as person can rehearse the words acoustically
  • Words from the end are put into Short-Term Memory
  • Words in the middle of the list had been there too long to be held in Short-Term Memory and not long enough to be transferred to Long-Term Memory
