1 | Basic economic problem: choice and the allocation of resources

The Basic Economic Problem

  • The basic economic problem:
    • Allocating scarce resources to satisfy unlimited needs and wants.
  • Factors of production:
    • Land: the natural resources required in the production process.
    • Labour: the human resources required in the production process.
    • Capital: the manufactured resources required in the production process.
    • Enterprise: the skills a business person requires to combine and manage successfully the other three factors of production and the ability to undertake risk.
  • Opportunity Cost:
    • the next best alternative foregone.
  • Production Possibility Curves (PPC)
    • represents the productive capacity (maximum output) of the economy.
